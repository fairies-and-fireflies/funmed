FAQ

Can I make Renaissance Health my primary care office?

Should you have a primary care physician?  Yes.  Your PCP is important in addressing acute illness and managing serious chronic illness.  They should be available to offer routine vaccinations and screenings in the office as well as evaluate acute illness, order urgent labs and imaging, and offer on-call services.  

Can you prescribe medicines?

Absolutely!  However, if there are tools available that can bring you back into balance and correct dysfunction that do not involve daily medication we will explore those options as well.  We suggest supplements from trusted sources that are tested and vetted to ensure quality and safety.

Does Renaissance Health accept insurance?

Not at this time.  Insurance restricts the length of patient visits and does not reimburse for health counseling or preventative medicine.  You can request a superbill to submit to your insurance for reimbursement directly to you.  All payments are due at the time of service.  

What about labs?

This office has relationships with several free-standing labs in the area.  

Does Renaissance Health accept pediatric patients?

We absolutely love to see children! 

Are Renaissance Health services an HSA-allowable expense?

You will need to contact your benefits representative to find out if services from functional medicine practitioners are covered.
